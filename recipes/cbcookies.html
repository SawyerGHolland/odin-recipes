Creme Brulee Cookies

Description

Have you ever wanted the best Christmas cookies? If so, boy do we the best cookies that you have ever had. The sweetness and richness of creme brulee in cookie form is the best combination ever created. This is a recipe that will bring the whole family together around the table.

We all want to have a recipe that we pass down from generation to generation. This is that recipe! The first time I made this recipe for my family it was all they could talk about. Next year it was all they were asking for. They didn't care if I were there, they just wanted the cookies there. This recipe will have your family worried more about food than you.

Ingredients

For The Cookies

  • 3/4 c. (1 1/2 sticks) butter, softened
  • 1/2 c. packed brown sugar
  • 1/2 c. granulated sugar
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 tbsp. pure vanilla extract
  • 2 c. all-purpose flour
  • 2 tsp. cornstarch
  • 1 tsp. baking soda
  • 1/4 tsp. salt

For the Frosting

  • 1 (8-oz.) block creme cheese, softened
  • 1 1/4 c. powdered sugar
  • 1 tsp. pure vanilla extract
  • 1/4 c. granulated sugar

Steps

  1. Preheat oven to 350 degrees and line cookie sheet with parchment. Cream butter and sugar together until light and fluffy, 3-4 minutes. Mix egg and vanilla.
  2. In another bowl, whisk dry ingredients then add to the wet ingredients and mix until smooth. Dough will be thick.
  3. Using a spoon, get a spoonful and roll into a ball with your hands. Dough should be about a 1-2 inches wide and place onto cookie sheet. Using the same spoon, smush the top of the cookie so it is flat.
  4. Place cookie sheet into the oven and let it bake for 9-10 minutes.
  5. Remove from the oven and let the cookies cool for a minute before moving them to a cooling rack to finish cooling.
  6. Mix frosting while waiting for cookies to finish cooling.
  7. Start with beating the creme cheese until smooth. Then add powdered sugar and vanilla until smooth.
  8. Put sugar into a small bowl. Spread the frosting onto the cookies ( using a piping bag and then smooting with a knife is best). Then take the cookie and press the frosting into the sugar coating the frosting.
  9. Just before serving, use a kitchen torch to caramelize the sugar on top of the cookie. Store cookies in the fridge up to 4 days.
